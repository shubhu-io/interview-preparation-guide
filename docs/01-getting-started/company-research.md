## Introduction

**Company research** is the systematic process of gathering, analyzing, and synthesizing information about a target employer before applying or attending an interview. Interviewers consistently rank "lack of company knowledge" among the top reasons candidates fail.

When you demonstrate genuine understanding of a company's challenges, products, and culture, you:
- Show genuine interest beyond just getting a job
- Answer behavioral questions with company-specific context
- Ask insightful questions that impress interviewers
- Negotiate offers from a position of knowledge

## Key Concepts

### Analysis Frameworks

| Framework | Components | Use Case |
|-----------|-----------|----------|
| **PESTEL** | Political, Economic, Social, Technological, Environmental, Legal | Macro-environment analysis |
| **SWOT** | Strengths, Weaknesses, Opportunities, Threats | Internal/external assessment |
| **Porter's Five Forces** | New entrants, Suppliers, Buyers, Substitutes, Rivalry | Industry competitiveness |

### Key Concepts Table

| Concept | Definition | Interview Application |
|---------|------------|----------------------|
| Business Model | How the company creates, delivers, and captures value | "How does the company make money?" |
| Value Proposition | Unique benefit the company offers customers | "What makes this company different?" |
| Market Position | Company's standing relative to competitors | "Where does the company fit?" |
| Culture Fit | Alignment between candidate and company values | Behavioral questions |
| Competitive Advantage | What makes the company better than rivals | "What are the company's strengths?" |
| Financial Health | Company's monetary stability | Negotiating offers |
| Strategic Direction | Company's future plans and vision | Aligning your goals with theirs |

## Frequently Asked Interview Questions

### Beginner

**Q: Tell me about our company.**
I know [Company] was founded in [year] and has grown to become a leader in [industry]. Your mission is [mission statement], and you're particularly known for [key product/service]. I was impressed by [recent achievement].

**Q: What products or services does our company offer?**
Your primary products include [Product A] which serves [target market], [Product B] that addresses [specific need], and your newer offering [Product C] which shows innovation in [area].

**Q: Who are our main competitors?**
Your primary competitors are [Competitor A] who focuses on [strength], [Competitor B] known for [niche], and [Competitor C] which has [position]. What sets [Company] apart is [unique value proposition].

**Q: Why do you want to work here?**
Beyond the role itself, I'm drawn to [Company] because [specific reason from research]. Your recent [initiative/product] shows commitment to [value], which aligns with my professional goal.

**Q: What do you know about our culture?**
From my research, I understand [Company] values [core values]. I read on Glassdoor that employees particularly appreciate [positive aspect]. Your [program] also demonstrates investment in [benefit].

### Intermediate

**Q: What challenges do you think our company faces?**
I see a few key challenges: [industry challenge], [specific company challenge] given [conditions], and [competitive challenge]. However, [Company]'s [strength] positions well to address these.

**Q: How has the company evolved recently?**
I've noticed several significant changes: [Product launch], [Leadership change], [Strategic pivot], and [Funding/expansion]. The [specific change] particularly interests me because [reason].

**Q: What trends do you see in our industry?**
Key trends include [trend 1] driven by [factor], [trend 2] as evidenced by [example], and [trend 3] which [Company] is well-positioned to capitalize on.

**Q: How would you improve our product/service?**
Based on user reviews and competitive analysis, I see opportunities in [improvement area]. For instance, [suggestion] could address [user pain point].

### Advanced

**Q: If you were our CEO, what would be your top 3 priorities?**
First, [strategic priority] because [analysis supports this]. Second, [operational priority] to address [challenge]. Third, [innovation priority] to capitalize on [opportunity].

**Q: Analyze our competitive position in the market.**
[Company] holds [position] with [strengths]. However, [competitors] are strong in [areas]. The market is moving toward [trend], where [Company]'s [advantage] is significant.

**Q: What would you do in your first 90 days?**
Days 1-30: Learning phase — understand team, processes, and priorities. Days 31-60: Contribution phase — apply learning to specific areas. Days 61-90: Impact phase — deliver results while building relationships.

## Common Mistakes

| Mistake | Why It's Bad | How to Fix |
|---------|--------------|------------|
| Only knowing basic facts | Surface-level research | Deep dive into business model |
| Memorizing Wikipedia facts | Sounds rehearsed | Synthesize in your own words |
| Ignoring recent news | Not current | Set up Google Alerts |
| Not researching competitors | Misses market context | Create competitive comparison |
| Overlooking culture | May misjudge fit | Read employee reviews |
| Not knowing interview process | Lack of preparation | Research on Glassdoor |
| Ignoring financial health | May miss warning signs | Review financial reports |
| Not tailoring questions | Generic shows disinterest | Prepare role-specific questions |

## Best Practices

1. **Start Early** — Begin research at least 2-3 weeks before interview
2. **Use Multiple Sources** — Cross-reference from different platforms
3. **Take Structured Notes** — Use templates to organize findings
4. **Focus on Relevance** — Prioritize info related to your target role
5. **Stay Current** — Focus on recent developments (last 6-12 months)
6. **Synthesize, Don't Memorize** — Understand themes, not details
7. **Prepare Stories** — Connect your experience to company challenges
8. **Ask Thoughtful Questions** — Use research to generate genuine curiosity

## Quick Reference

### Research Checklist

- [ ] Founded, HQ, CEO, employee count
- [ ] Revenue, growth rate, funding status
- [ ] Key products/services
- [ ] Mission, vision, and values
- [ ] 3-5 main competitors
- [ ] Recent news (last 6 months)
- [ ] Culture assessment (Glassdoor reviews)
- [ ] Interview process and common questions
- [ ] Prepared 5-7 thoughtful questions

### Key Financial Metrics to Know

| Metric | What It Is | Why It Matters |
|--------|-----------|----------------|
| TAM | Total Addressable Market | Market opportunity size |
| CAC | Customer Acquisition Cost | Growth efficiency |
| LTV | Customer Lifetime Value | Revenue per customer |
| Burn Rate | Capital spending rate | Company sustainability |
| Runway | Time before running out of money | Startup viability |
| P/E Ratio | Price-to-Earnings | Valuation metric |

### Research Sources Hierarchy

| Priority | Source | Data Type |
|----------|--------|-----------|
| Primary | Company website, SEC filings, press releases | Official, verified |
| Secondary | News articles, analyst reports, industry reports | Analysis, context |
| Tertiary | Glassdoor, Wikipedia, Crunchbase, LinkedIn | Reviews, background |
