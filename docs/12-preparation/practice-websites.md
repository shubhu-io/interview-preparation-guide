# Practice Websites for Interview Preparation

Online practice platforms provide structured environments to hone coding skills, solve problems, and track progress.

## Introduction

Practice websites offer curated problem sets, coding environments, and communities to support interview preparation. Choosing the right platforms and using them effectively accelerates learning.

## Key Concepts

### Platform Categories
| Category | Examples | Best For |
|----------|----------|----------|
| Coding Practice | LeetCode, HackerRank | DSA problems |
| System Design | Educative, ByteByteGo | Architecture |
| Mock Interviews | Pramp, Interviewing.io | Live practice |
| Courses | Coursera, Udemy | Structured learning |
| Community | Stack Overflow, Reddit | Q&A, support |

### Top Platforms by Topic
- **DSA**: LeetCode, GeeksforGeeks, HackerRank
- **System Design**: Educative, ByteByteGo, SystemDesign.one
- **Behavioral**: Pramp, Interviewing.io
- **SQL**: HackerRank, LeetCode Database
- **Full Stack**: Codecademy, freeCodeCamp

### Usage Strategy
1. Primary platform for main practice
2. Secondary for variety
3. Mock interview platform for live practice
4. Course platform for structured learning
5. Community for support and Q&A

### Progress Tracking Features
- Problem count and difficulty distribution
- Acceptance rates
- Contest rankings
- Streak tracking
- Topic-wise progress

## FAQ

### Q1: Which platform is best for DSA?
LeetCode is the industry standard. Supplement with GeeksforGeeks.

### Q2: How many problems should I solve?
200-300 problems across all difficulty levels.

### Q3: Should I use multiple platforms?
Yes. 2-3 platforms for variety and different perspectives.

### Q4: Are premium subscriptions worth it?
LeetCode premium is worth it for company-specific questions.

### Q5: Should I follow a study plan?
Yes. Most platforms offer structured plans. Follow one.

### Q6: How do I track my progress?
Use built-in tracking, maintain a spreadsheet, or use Notion.

### Q7: Should I do contests?
Yes. Weekly contests build speed and pressure handling.

### Q8: How do I choose problems?
Follow a study plan or focus on weak areas.

### Q9: Should I discuss problems with others?
Yes. Communities provide different perspectives and solutions.

### Q10: How do I avoid burnout?
Vary platforms, take breaks, and mix difficulty levels.

## Common Mistakes

1. **Using too many platforms** - Focus on 2-3 main ones
2. **Not tracking progress** - Can't improve what you don't measure
3. **Only doing easy problems** - Challenge yourself
4. **Not reviewing solutions** - Learn from every problem
5. **Ignoring time complexity** - Always analyze and optimize
6. **Not practicing under pressure** - Time yourself

## Best Practices

1. Choose 2-3 primary platforms
2. Follow a structured study plan
3. Track progress consistently
4. Review all solutions
5. Participate in contests
6. Discuss with communities
7. Focus on weak areas
8. Balance difficulty levels
