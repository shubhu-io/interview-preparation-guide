# Final Revision Before Interview Day

Final revision in the last 24-48 hours before your interview consolidates knowledge and builds confidence without overloading your mind.

## Introduction

Final revision is about consolidation, not cramming. The goal is to review key concepts, build confidence, and rest your mind. What you do in the last day matters less than what you've done in the weeks prior.

## Key Concepts

### Final Day Timeline (24-48 hours before)
| Time | Activity |
|------|----------|
| 48-36 hours | Light review of cheat sheets, notes |
| 36-24 hours | Review key algorithms, patterns, stories |
| 24-12 hours | Stop new learning, light review only |
| 12-4 hours | Relax, sleep, no studying |
| 4-0 hours | Get ready, arrive early, relax |

### What to Review
- Cheat sheets (quick scan)
- Key algorithms and patterns
- STAR stories
- System design patterns
- Company research

### What NOT to Do
- Cram new material
- Solve difficult problems
- Study past your bedtime
- Panic about forgotten material
- Skip sleep for more study

### Pre-Interview Confidence Builders
- Review achievements and strengths
- Remember preparation done
- Visualize success
- Practice breathing exercises
- Trust your preparation

## FAQ

### Q1: Should I study the day before?
Light review only. Focus on cheat sheets and key concepts.

### Q2: Should I solve problems the day before?
No. Solve problems earlier in the week, not the final day.

### Q3: How long should I study on final day?
1-2 hours maximum. Rest is more important.

### Q4: What if I'm panicking?
Breathe, remember your preparation, trust the process, and rest.

### Q5: Should I sleep early the night before?
Yes. 7-8 hours of sleep is essential for performance.

### Q6: What should I do the morning of the interview?
Light breakfast, arrive early, review key points briefly, breathe.

### Q7: Should I bring cheat sheets to review at the office?
No. They're for preparation at home, not for use at the interview.

### Q8: How do I handle pre-interview nerves?
Breathing exercises, visualization, remembering preparation, and arriving early.

### Q9: What if I realize I forgot something?
Accept it. Focus on what you know, not what you don't.

### Q10: How do I stay confident?
Remember your preparation, trust yourself, and visualize success.

## Common Mistakes

1. **Cramming the night before** - Cramming creates anxiety, not knowledge
2. **Studying too late** - Sleep deprivation hurts performance
3. **Not eating/sleeping well** - Physical state affects mental performance
4. **Panicking about forgotten material** - Trust your preparation
5. **Not preparing logistics** - Plan route, outfit, documents
6. **Negative self-talk** - Focus on strengths, not weaknesses

## Best Practices

1. Review only, don't cram
2. Sleep well the night before
3. Eat healthy, hydrate
4. Arrive early to the venue
5. Trust your preparation
6. Breathe and stay calm
7. Visualize success
8. Remember your strengths

## Quick Reference

### Final Day Checklist
```
□ Review cheat sheets (30 min)
□ Light review of key concepts (30 min)
□ Prepare outfit and documents
□ Plan route to interview venue
□ Set alarm with buffer time
□ Get 7-8 hours of sleep
□ Eat healthy breakfast
□ Arrive 15-20 minutes early
```

### Breathing Exercise
```
Inhale 4 seconds
Hold 4 seconds
Exhale 4 seconds
Hold 4 seconds
Repeat 4 times
```

### What to Bring
```
□ Resume (2 copies)
□ Portfolio/notebook
□ List of questions
□ ID/pen
□ Snack/water
□ Confidence
```
