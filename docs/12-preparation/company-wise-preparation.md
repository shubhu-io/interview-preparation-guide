# Company-wise Preparation Strategy

Tailoring preparation to specific companies increases your chances of success by aligning your skills with their requirements.

- [Introduction](#introduction)

## Introduction

Different companies have different interview styles, focus areas, and difficulty levels. Company-specific preparation helps you align your efforts with what matters most to each employer.

## Key Concepts

### Company Categories
| Category | Examples | Focus |
|----------|----------|-------|
| FAANG | Google, Amazon, Meta, Apple, Netflix | DSA, System Design |
| MNCs | Microsoft, IBM, Oracle | DSA, OOP, System Design |
| Startups | Various | Full-stack, versatility |
| Product Companies | Uber, Airbnb, Stripe | DSA, System Design |
| Service Companies | TCS, Infosys, Wipro | Aptitude, basics |

### Company-Specific Preparation Areas
1. **Tech Stack**: Languages and frameworks they use
2. **Problem Patterns**: Types of problems they ask
3. **Culture**: Values and work environment
4. **Interview Process**: Number of rounds, format
5. **Recent Projects**: What they're building

### Preparation Strategy by Company Type
- **FAANG**: Focus on DSA (medium-hard), system design, behavioral
- **Startups**: Full-stack skills, versatility, cultural fit
- **Service Companies**: Aptitude, basic DSA, communication
- **Product Companies**: DSA, system design, domain expertise

### Research Sources
- Company career pages
- Glassdoor reviews
- Blind app
- LinkedIn profiles
- Tech blogs
- Interview experiences on GeeksforGeeks

## FAQ

### Q1: How do I find out what a company asks?
Check interview experiences on GeeksforGeeks, Glassdoor, Blind, and LinkedIn.

### Q2: Should I prepare differently for each company?
Yes. Tailor preparation to their tech stack, culture, and interview style.

### Q3: How do I research company culture?
Check career page, Glassdoor, LinkedIn, and company blogs.

### Q4: Should I apply to multiple companies?
Yes. Apply to 10-15 companies to maximize chances.

### Q5: How do I prepare for startups vs FAANG?
Startups: versatility, full-stack. FAANG: depth, DSA, system design.

### Q6: Should I prepare for company-specific questions?
Yes. Research their products, tech stack, and recent projects.

### Q7: How do I find the right level of preparation?
Check interview experiences for difficulty levels and adjust accordingly.

### Q8: Should I network with employees?
Yes. Reach out for referrals and insights.

### Q9: How do I prioritize which companies to prepare for?
Focus on companies aligned with your skills, interests, and career goals.

### Q10: Should I prepare for behavioral questions company-specifically?
Yes. Align your stories with their values and leadership principles.

## Common Mistakes

1. **Generic preparation** - Tailor to specific companies
2. **Not researching company culture** - Fit matters
3. **Ignoring company tech stack** - Prepare relevant skills
4. **Not networking** - Referrals help
5. **Applying to too few companies** - Diversify
6. **Not tracking applications** - Stay organized

## Best Practices

1. Research each company thoroughly
2. Tailor preparation to their requirements
3. Practice company-specific questions
4. Network for referrals and insights
5. Apply to multiple companies
6. Track applications and follow up
7. Prepare company-specific stories
8. Stay updated on company news

## Quick Reference

### Company Research Checklist
```
□ Tech stack and frameworks
□ Interview process and rounds
□ Recent projects and products
□ Company culture and values
□ Glassdoor reviews and ratings
□ Salary ranges and benefits
□ Growth opportunities
□ Team structure
```

### Application Strategy
```
Target: 10-15 companies
Timeline: 2-3 months
Focus: 3-4 high-priority
Backup: 5-6 safety options
Reach: 2-3 stretch goals
```
