# Weekly Revision for Interview Preparation

Weekly revision provides structured time to review the week's learning, assess progress, identify gaps, and plan the upcoming week.

## Introduction

While daily study builds knowledge incrementally, weekly revision ensures nothing falls through the cracks and keeps your preparation on track. It's the checkpoint that aligns daily efforts with overall goals.

## Key Concepts

### Weekly Review Components
1. **Progress Assessment**: Review daily metrics, compare to goals
2. **Knowledge Review**: Test yourself on key concepts
3. **Weak Area Identification**: Which topics need more attention?
4. **Planning**: Set goals for next week, schedule mocks

### Balanced Coverage (40-30-20-10 Rule)
- **40%**: Weak areas (most improvement potential)
- **30%**: High-frequency interview topics
- **20%**: Medium-frequency topics
- **10%**: Low-frequency topics

### Weekly Self-Assessment Areas
1. Technical Proficiency (1-10)
2. Conceptual Understanding (1-10)
3. Communication (1-10)
4. Time Management (1-10)
5. Confidence (1-10)

### Weekend Mock Interviews
- **Saturday**: Technical mock (coding + system design)
- **Sunday**: Behavioral mock (STAR stories + LPs)

## FAQ

### Q1: How long should a weekly review be?
1-2 hours on a weekend day. Focus on quality review.

### Q2: Should I do mock interviews every week?
Yes, 1-2 mocks per week is ideal. Alternate between technical and behavioral.

### Q3: Should I change my daily routine based on weekly review?
Yes. If review reveals gaps, adjust daily focus areas accordingly.

### Q4: What's the best day for weekly review?
Usually Sunday, but choose a day when you're fresh and focused.

### Q5: How do I assess progress weekly?
Review daily metrics, compare to goals, test yourself, and rate confidence.

### Q6: What if I'm not meeting weekly goals?
Adjust goals to be more realistic, identify barriers, and modify your approach.

### Q7: Should I track weekly progress?
Absolutely. Weekly tracking shows trends and helps adjust your plan.

### Q8: How do I handle busy weeks?
Do a minimal review session. Even 30 minutes maintains momentum.

### Q9: What's the most important part of weekly review?
Identifying weak areas and adjusting your plan accordingly.

### Q10: Should I adjust weekly goals over time?
Yes. As you improve, increase goals. If overwhelmed, reduce them.

## Common Mistakes

1. **Skipping weekly reviews** - Essential for course correction
2. **Not adjusting based on review** - Review without action is wasted
3. **Too many mock interviews** - 1-2 per week is sufficient
4. **Ignoring weak areas** - Weekly review should address gaps
5. **Not tracking progress** - Can't assess improvement without tracking
6. **Unrealistic weekly goals** - Lead to burnout

## Best Practices

1. Schedule it - block time on your calendar
2. Be consistent - same day and time each week
3. Review AND plan - assess this week, plan next
4. Track progress with metrics
5. Focus on weaknesses
6. Include regular mock interviews
7. Adjust based on results
8. Celebrate wins to maintain motivation

## Quick Reference

### Weekly Review Session (1-2 hours)
```
Review daily logs (15 min)
Assess progress vs goals (15 min)
Test key concepts (20 min)
Identify weak areas (10 min)
Plan next week (20 min)
```

### Assessment Scoring
```
1-3: Needs significant work
4-6: Developing
7-8: Strong
9-10: Excellent
```
