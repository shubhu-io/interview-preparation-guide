# Monthly Revision for Interview Preparation

Monthly revision provides broader strategic review, helping you assess overall progress and make significant adjustments to your preparation strategy.

## Introduction

While daily and weekly revisions focus on tactical learning, monthly revision allows you to step back and evaluate your overall strategy, identify systemic issues, and make meaningful course corrections.

## Key Concepts

### Monthly Review Components
1. **Progress Analysis**: Review weekly metrics, identify trends
2. **Strategy Assessment**: Is your approach working?
3. **Goal Adjustment**: Update short and long-term goals
4. **Gap Analysis**: Which topics need more focus?
5. **Timeline Check**: Are you on track for interview dates?

### Monthly Assessment Framework
| Area | Review Questions |
|------|-----------------|
| Technical | Are DSA scores improving? Which patterns are weak? |
| Behavioral | Are stories compelling? Are LPs well-developed? |
| Communication | Is articulation clear? Are you concise? |
| Confidence | Is confidence growing? Where is doubt? |
| Timeline | On track for interview dates? |

### Monthly Progress Indicators
- Problems solved this month vs last
- Mock interview scores
- Concept understanding ratings
- Confidence levels
- Time to solution (speed improvement)

### Course Correction Strategies
- **If behind**: Increase study time or reduce scope
- **If ahead**: Add depth or new topics
- **If weak areas persist**: Seek different resources or mentorship
- **If burnout**: Take a break or reduce intensity

## FAQ

### Q1: How long should monthly review be?
2-3 hours on a weekend. Use this time for deep reflection and planning.

### Q2: What metrics should I track monthly?
Problems solved, mock scores, confidence ratings, topic coverage.

### Q3: How do I adjust goals monthly?
Review progress, compare to timeline, and update goals based on reality.

### Q4: What if I'm not improving?
Identify barriers, change approach, seek help, or adjust timeline.

### Q5: Should I change my study plan monthly?
Yes. Monthly review should inform adjustments to your plan.

### Q6: How do I measure improvement?
Compare monthly metrics, track mock scores, and assess confidence.

### Q7: What if I need more time?
Push back interview dates if possible, or focus on highest-impact areas.

### Q8: Should I take a full day off monthly?
Yes. Rest is essential for sustained performance.

### Q9: How do I handle plateaus?
Change approach, seek new resources, take a break, or focus on different areas.

### Q10: What's the most important monthly review output?
An updated, realistic plan for the next month.

## Common Mistakes

1. **Not reviewing monthly** - Essential for strategic adjustment
2. **Ignoring trends** - Patterns reveal systemic issues
3. **Not adjusting goals** - Stale goals lead to frustration
4. **Overanalyzing** - Paralysis by analysis
5. **Ignoring burnout** - Sustainable pace matters
6. **Not celebrating progress** - Acknowledge wins

## Best Practices

1. Schedule monthly reviews - block time on calendar
2. Review weekly metrics - look for trends
3. Assess objectively - be honest about progress
4. Adjust realistically - set achievable goals
5. Plan next month - specific actions and goals
6. Take breaks - rest is part of success
7. Seek feedback - external perspective helps
8. Celebrate wins - acknowledge progress

## Quick Reference

### Monthly Review Session (2-3 hours)
```
Review weekly logs (30 min)
Assess progress vs goals (30 min)
Identify trends and patterns (30 min)
Adjust goals and plan (30 min)
Plan next month (30 min)
Celebrate wins (15 min)
```

### Progress Indicators
```
Problems solved: +10-15% monthly
Mock scores: +5-10% monthly
Confidence: +1 point monthly
Topic coverage: +10-15% monthly
```
