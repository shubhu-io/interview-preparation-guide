# Personal Notes for Interview Preparation

Effective note-taking organizes knowledge, reinforces learning, and creates quick reference materials for interview preparation.

## Introduction

Good notes process, organize, and retain knowledge in a way that's useful for your specific goals. They serve as personalized study materials for quick review.

## Key Concepts

### Note-Taking Methods

| Method | Structure | Best For |
|--------|-----------|----------|
| Cornell | 3-column (notes, questions, summary) | Active learning, review |
| Mind Mapping | Visual, radiating branches | Creative thinking, connections |
| Outline | Hierarchical indentation | Structured subjects |
| Charting | Columns and rows | Comparison, factual info |

### Digital Tools
- **Notion**: All-in-one workspace
- **Obsidian**: Markdown-based, local-first
- **Anki**: Spaced repetition flashcards
- **OneNote**: Microsoft ecosystem

### Active Note-Taking
- Summarize in your own words
- Connect to existing knowledge
- Question assumptions
- Apply concepts to problems
- Review regularly

### Spaced Repetition for Notes
- Day 1: Initial note-taking
- Day 2: First review (5 minutes)
- Day 7: Second review (3 minutes)
- Day 30: Third review (2 minutes)
- Day 90: Final review (1 minute)

## FAQ

### Q1: What's the best note-taking method?
Cornell method for learning new topics, mind mapping for connections, outlines for structured review.

### Q2: Should I take notes by hand or digitally?
Both have benefits. Handwriting improves retention; digital enables search and organization.

### Q3: How do I organize notes for multiple topics?
Use consistent folder structure, tagging system, and index. Review and reorganize regularly.

### Q4: How often should I review notes?
Daily for active topics, weekly for maintenance, monthly for comprehensive review.

### Q5: Should I rewrite notes?
Yes. Rewriting forces you to process and organize information.

### Q6: What's the Zettelkasten method?
A system of interconnected notes where each note is a single idea, linked to related notes.

### Q7: How do I create effective cheat sheets?
Focus on key formulas, patterns, and concepts. Use visual formatting for quick scanning.

### Q8: Should I take notes during coding practice?
Yes. Note patterns, common mistakes, and solutions for future reference.

### Q9: What tools are best for note organization?
Notion for all-in-one, Obsidian for local-first, Anki for flashcards.

### Q10: How do I use notes during interviews?
You don't use notes during interviews. Review them before to prepare.

## Common Mistakes

1. **Taking verbatim notes** - Summarize instead of copying
2. **Never reviewing notes** - Schedule regular review sessions
3. **Not connecting concepts** - Show relationships between ideas
4. **Using too many tools** - Choose 2-3 tools and master them
5. **Not making notes actionable** - Focus on what you'll use for review
6. **Perfectionism** - Don't spend more time making notes look perfect than learning

## Best Practices

1. Process, don't copy - summarize in your own words
2. Connect ideas - link related concepts together
3. Review regularly - schedule consistent review sessions
4. Use visuals - diagrams and charts enhance memory
5. Create cheat sheets - one-page summaries for quick review
6. Organize consistently - use standard structure and tagging
7. Share when possible - teaching reinforces learning
