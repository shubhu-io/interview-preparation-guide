## FAQ

### Q1: What's the most important resource?
"Cracking the Coding Interview" for DSA, "System Design Interview" for architecture.

### Q2: Should I buy books or use online resources?
Both. Books for depth, online for practice and variety.

### Q3: Are free resources sufficient?
Yes. Many excellent free resources exist. Premium adds convenience, not quality.

### Q4: How do I choose the right course?
Check reviews, preview content, and align with your learning style.

### Q5: Should I join a bootcamp?
If you need structure and accountability, yes. Self-study works for disciplined learners.

### Q6: How do I stay updated on new resources?
Follow tech communities, subscribe to newsletters, and check review sites.

### Q7: Should I use multiple resources for the same topic?
Yes. Different perspectives reinforce learning.

### Q8: How do I manage resource overload?
Focus on 2-3 high-quality resources per topic. Depth beats breadth.

### Q9: Are video courses better than books?
Depends on your learning style. Videos for visual, books for reading.

### Q10: Should I contribute to open source?
Yes. It builds skills, provides experience, and strengthens your profile.

## Common Mistakes

1. **Resource overload** - Too many resources dilute focus
2. **Not using resources actively** - Reading ≠ learning
3. **Ignoring free resources** - Many excellent free options exist
4. **Not reviewing resources** - Rate and share
5. **Using outdated resources** - Check publication dates
6. **Not matching learning style** - Choose resources that fit you

## Best Practices

1. Curate quality resources
2. Match resources to learning style
3. Use resources actively (practice, not just read)
4. Review and rate resources
5. Share with peers
6. Update regularly
7. Focus on depth over breadth
8. Balance free and paid resources
