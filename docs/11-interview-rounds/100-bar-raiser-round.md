# Bar Raiser Round

The Bar Raiser round is an independent evaluation designed to ensure hiring quality stays high. A Bar Raiser is someone outside the hiring team who has veto power and focuses on whether the candidate raises the overall talent bar.

## Introduction

Bar Raiser rounds exist to prevent hiring bias and ensure every new hire makes the team stronger. The Bar Raiser has no stake in filling the position quickly — their sole focus is whether you're better than the average person currently in the role.

This round is often the most independent and objective evaluation in the process. The Bar Raiser doesn't care about team pressure or urgency — they care about quality.

## Key Concepts

### The Bar Raiser's Role

- **Independent evaluation** — No relationship to the hiring team
- **Quality gatekeeper** — Ensures every hire raises the bar
- **Veto power** — Can block a hire even if the team wants to proceed
- **Bias reduction** — Provides an objective perspective
- **Standard enforcement** — Applies consistent evaluation criteria

### What Bar Raisers Evaluate

**Competency Depth**
- Can you demonstrate expertise in your domain?
- Do you have a track record of excellence?
- Can you handle the complexity of this role?

**Growth Trajectory**
- Are you growing faster than your peers?
- Do you show potential for greater impact?
- Will you grow in this role?

**Culture Contribution**
- Will you add to the company culture?
- Do you demonstrate the company's values?
- Will others want to work with you?

**Hiring Bar Assessment**
- Are you better than the average person in this role?
- Would you raise the overall team capability?
- Is there evidence of exceptional performance?

### Common Bar Raiser Questions

- Tell me about a time you went above and beyond
- Describe a situation where you significantly raised the bar
- What's the most complex problem you've solved?
- How do you ensure quality in your work?
- Tell me about a time you pushed for excellence when others were satisfied
- What would your peers say distinguishes you from them?

### The Bar Raiser Evaluation Framework

**Signal Assessment**
- Strong signals: Consistent evidence of excellence
- Mixed signals: Some strong, some weak areas
- Weak signals: Insufficient evidence of raising the bar

**Calibration**
- Comparing to the current team average
- Assessing growth potential
- Evaluating unique value addition

**Decision Framework**
- Strong hire: Clearly raises the bar
- Lean hire: Probably raises the bar, some concerns
- No hire: Doesn't clearly raise the bar

### How to Demonstrate You Raise the Bar

**Show Exceptional Impact**
- Quantify results that exceed expectations
- Share examples of going beyond your role
- Demonstrate measurable improvement in team or product

**Demonstrate Growth**
- Show a trajectory of increasing responsibility
- Share how you've developed new skills
- Explain how you've adapted to new challenges

**Prove Culture Addition**
- Share values-aligned examples
- Show how you've positively influenced team culture
- Demonstrate collaboration and leadership

## FAQ

**Q: What makes the Bar Raiser round different?**
A: It's independent, objective, and focused solely on whether you raise the overall hiring bar.

**Q: Can the Bar Raiser block my hiring?**
A: Yes. Bar Raisers have veto power to maintain hiring quality.

**Q: How do I prepare for this round?**
A: Focus on demonstrating exceptional impact and growth. Show you're clearly above average.

**Q: What if I'm nervous about this round?**
A: Remember it's an objective evaluation. Focus on demonstrating your genuine capabilities.

**Q: Should I try to impress the Bar Raiser?**
A: Focus on being authentic and demonstrating real impact. Impression management is less important than evidence.

**Q: What if I don't have exceptional examples?**
A: Focus on consistent excellence and growth trajectory. Raising the bar doesn't always mean being extraordinary — it means being clearly above average.

**Q: How do I show I'll add to the culture?**
A: Share examples that demonstrate the company's values in action. Be authentic about your working style.

**Q: What if the Bar Raiser asks about weaknesses?**
A: Be honest. Bar Raisers value self-awareness and growth mindset.

**Q: Should I ask the Bar Raiser questions?**
A: Yes. Thoughtful questions show genuine interest and strategic thinking.

**Q: How does the Bar Raiser decide?**
A: They evaluate whether you clearly raise the bar compared to the current team average.

## Common Mistakes

- **Being average** — The whole point is demonstrating you're above average
- **Being inauthentic** — Bar Raisers can detect pretense
- **Not showing growth** — Trajectory matters as much as current capability
- **Ignoring culture** — Culture addition is a key evaluation criterion
- **Being defensive** — Bar Raisers may challenge you to test composure
- **Not quantifying impact** — Numbers demonstrate real value
- **Focusing only on skills** — Show the full picture of who you are

## Best Practices

1. **Focus on exceptional impact** — Show results that exceed expectations
2. **Demonstrate growth** — Show a trajectory of increasing capability
3. **Prove culture addition** — Share values-aligned examples
4. **Quantify everything** — Numbers demonstrate real value
5. **Be authentic** — Bar Raisers value genuine self-awareness
6. **Show consistency** — Consistent excellence beats occasional brilliance
7. **Prepare for challenges** — Bar Raisers may test your composure
8. **Ask thoughtful questions** — Show genuine interest in the company and role
