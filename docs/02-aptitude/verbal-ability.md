# Verbal Ability and Comprehension

{: .note }
Verbal ability demonstrates communication skills and is essential for client-facing and leadership roles.

## Introduction

Verbal ability refers to the capacity to understand, use, and manipulate language effectively. It encompasses grammar, vocabulary, reading comprehension, sentence structure, and communication skills.

**Why It Matters:**
- Core component of most aptitude tests
- Demonstrates communication skills
- Essential for client-facing and leadership roles
- Used to evaluate writing and comprehension abilities

## Key Areas

| Area | Focus | Importance |
|------|-------|------------|
| Grammar | Sentence structure, tenses, agreement | Very High |
| Vocabulary | Synonyms, antonyms, word meanings | High |

## Grammar Fundamentals

### Parts of Speech
1. **Noun**: Person, place, thing, idea
2. **Pronoun**: Replaces a noun
3. **Verb**: Action or state of being
4. **Adjective**: Modifies a noun
5. **Adverb**: Modifies a verb, adjective, or adverb
6. **Preposition**: Shows relationship
7. **Conjunction**: Connects words/phrases

### Tenses
| Tense | Structure | Example |
|-------|-----------|---------|
| Simple Present | V/Vs | He plays |
| Present Continuous | is/am/are + Ving | He is playing |
| Present Perfect | has/have + V3 | He has played |
| Simple Past | V2 | He played |
| Past Perfect | had + V3 | He had played |
| Simple Future | will + V | He will play |

### Subject-Verb Agreement
- Singular subject → Singular verb: He plays
- Plural subject → Plural verb: They play
- Collective nouns → Usually singular: The team plays

### Articles
- **A**: Before consonant sounds: a cat, a university
- **An**: Before vowel sounds: an apple, an hour
- **The**: Specific reference: the book, the sun

## Reading Comprehension Strategies

### Reading Strategies
1. **Skimming**: Quick overview for main ideas
2. **Scanning**: Quick search for specific information
3. **Active Reading**: Engaged comprehension

### Question Types
1. **Main Idea**: What is the passage mainly about?
2. **Supporting Details**: What details support the main idea?
3. **Inference**: What can be implied from the passage?
4. **Vocabulary in Context**: What does word X mean here?
5. **Author's Purpose**: Why did the author write this?

### Time Management
- Read passage once (3-4 minutes)
- Answer questions (1 minute each)
- Return to passage for specific details

## Sentence Correction

### Common Errors
1. **Subject-Verb Agreement**: The team is playing well
2. **Tense Consistency**: He went to the store and bought milk
3. **Pronoun Reference**: When John met Mike, John was happy
4. **Parallel Structure**: She likes swimming, running, and cycling
5. **Dangling Modifiers**: Walking to school, I got caught in the rain

### Correction Strategies
1. Read the entire sentence first
2. Identify the subject and verb
3. Check for agreement
4. Look for parallel structure
5. Eliminate redundant words

## Para Jumbles

### Key Words for Ordering
| Type | Examples |
|------|----------|
| Time | First, then, next, finally |
| Order | Firstly, secondly, subsequently |
| Addition | Also, moreover, furthermore |
| Contrast | However, but, yet, nevertheless |
| Cause | Because, since, as, therefore |

### Strategies
1. Find the opening sentence (introduces topic)
2. Look for pronouns (refer to earlier words)
3. Find concluding sentence
4. Use connectors (time words, transitions)
5. Check logical flow

## Best Practices

1. **Read extensively**: Improve vocabulary and comprehension
2. **Learn root words**: Build vocabulary systematically
3. **Practice grammar rules**: Master agreement, tenses, articles
4. **Use flashcards**: Learn new words daily
5. **Read diverse materials**: News, articles, books
6. **Practice timed passages**: Build speed and accuracy
7. **Review mistakes**: Understand why you got something wrong
8. **Seek feedback**: Have others check your writing

## Common Mistakes to Avoid

| Mistake | Impact | Solution |
|---------|--------|----------|
| Poor vocabulary | Can't understand passages | Build vocabulary systematically |
| Ignoring grammar rules | Sentence correction errors | Master basic grammar |
| Not reading actively | Poor comprehension | Engage with the text |
| Rushing through passages | Missing key details | Read carefully, take notes |
| Not practicing timed | Can't complete on time | Practice with time limits |

## Quick Reference

### Grammar Rules
- Subject-verb agreement: Singular subjects take singular verbs
- Article usage: A/an for general, the for specific
- Tense consistency: Maintain same tense within paragraph
- Pronoun agreement: Pronouns must agree with antecedents

### Vocabulary Tips
- Learn root words, prefixes, and suffixes
- Use context clues to figure out unknown words
- Practice using new words in sentences
- Review and test yourself regularly

### Reading Strategies
- Skim for main ideas first
- Scan for specific details
- Underline key points
- Summarize each paragraph mentally
