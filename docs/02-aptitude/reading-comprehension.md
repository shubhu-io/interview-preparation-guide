# Reading Comprehension for Interviews

{: .note }
Reading comprehension tests your ability to quickly grasp, analyze, and draw conclusions from written passages.

## Introduction

Reading comprehension is the ability to read, process, and understand the meaning of written text. It involves interpretation, inference, critical analysis, and retention of information.

**Why It Matters:**
- Core component of online assessments and aptitude tests
- Essential for case study discussions
- Required for document review tasks
- Signals ability to process complex information quickly

## Types of Passages

| Type | Characteristics | Strategy |
|------|----------------|----------|
| Narrative | Tells a story, chronological order | Track sequence of events |
| Expository | Explains a concept or process | Identify topic sentences |
| Argumentative | Presents and defends a position | Find claims, evidence, reasoning |
| Descriptive | Describes a scene, person, or thing | Note sensory details |
| Compare/Contrast | Examines similarities and differences | Track both sides systematically |
| Cause/Effect | Analyzes causes and outcomes | Map causal relationships |

## Question Types

### Main Idea Questions
**Signal words:** "mainly about," "best summarizes," "central idea"

**Strategy:** The main idea is usually in the first or last sentence of the passage, or the first sentence of each paragraph. It covers the ENTIRE passage.

**Common trap:** Answers that are too narrow or too broad.

### Inference Questions
**Signal words:** "implies," "suggests," "can be inferred"

**Strategy:** The correct answer is not directly stated but must be logically deduced from evidence in the passage.

### Detail Questions
**Signal words:** "according to the passage," "the author states"

**Strategy:** The answer is explicitly stated in the passage. Locate the specific section and match exactly.

### Tone & Purpose Questions
**Signal words:** "the author's attitude," "tone," "purpose"

**Strategy:** Look at word choice (positive, negative, neutral) and emotional language.

### Vocabulary in Context Questions
**Signal words:** "as used in the passage," "closest in meaning"

**Strategy:** Locate the word, read surrounding sentences, substitute each answer choice.

## Speed Reading Techniques

1. **Minimize Subvocalization**: Reduce inner voice reading each word
2. **Expand Eye Span**: Take in groups of 3-4 words at a time
3. **Reduce Regression**: Stop re-reading words already read
4. **Use a Pointer**: Guide eyes with finger or pen
5. **Chunking**: Group related words together

### Target Speeds
| Level | Speed (words/min) | Comprehension |
|-------|-------------------|---------------|
| Beginner | 150-250 | 70%+ |
| Average | 250-400 | 70%+ |
| Good | 400-600 | 60%+ |
| Excellent | 600-800+ | 60%+ |

## Active Reading Strategies

### SQ3R Method
1. **Survey**: Preview the passage (title, headings, structure)
2. **Question**: Turn headings into questions
3. **Read**: Read to answer your questions
4. **Recite**: Summarize each section in your own words
5. **Review**: Go back and review key points

### Passage Mapping
After reading, create a mental map:
- Paragraph 1: Topic = _____, Main point = _____
- Paragraph 2: Topic = _____, Main point = _____
- Overall: Main idea = _____, Author's position = _____

## Elimination Strategy

When stuck between answer choices, eliminate:
1. **Too extreme**: Words like "always," "never," "all," "none"
2. **Too narrow**: Only covers part of the passage
3. **Too broad**: Goes beyond what the passage discusses
4. **Not supported**: Can't be found or inferred from the passage
5. **Opposite**: Contradicts the passage

**Process of elimination increases accuracy from 25% to 50-75%.**

## Time Management

| Step | Action | Time per Passage |
|------|--------|-----------------|
| 1 | Survey passage | 0:30 |
| 2 | Active reading | 2:30 |
| 3 | Answer questions | 3:00 |
| 4 | Review flagged questions | 0:30 |
| **Total** | | **~6 minutes** |

**Rules:**
- Spend no more than 6-8 minutes per passage
- If a question takes more than 90 seconds, guess and move on
- Answer easier questions first within each passage

## Best Practices

1. **Read the passage once for comprehension**: Get main idea and structure
2. **Scan back for specific details**: Don't re-read entire passage
3. **Use elimination aggressively**: Rule out wrong answers
4. **Manage time carefully**: Don't spend too long on one question
5. **Practice daily**: Read diverse materials regularly
6. **Build vocabulary**: Learn new words in context
7. **Review wrong answers**: Understand why you got something wrong
8. **Stay calm**: Don't panic if passage seems difficult

## Common Mistakes to Avoid

| Mistake | Impact | Solution |
|---------|--------|----------|
| Re-reading entire passage | Wastes time | Scan back for specific details |
| Choosing extreme answers | Wrong answer | Eliminate "always," "never" options |
| Ignoring time limits | Can't finish | Practice with timed conditions |
| Not using elimination | Lower accuracy | Rule out wrong answers first |
| Relying on memory | Wrong answers | Go back to passage for details |
